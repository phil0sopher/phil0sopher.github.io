Background

During my time at BlueJay Mobile Health, I was tasked with solving the digital health business’ and their users’ needs. During this time:

  1. I conducted and synthesized both user and marketing research
  2. I designed the payment flow of the product and marketing material
  3. I designed and developed their company’s website framework

Getting Started

Since I was new to the company and our audience, I decided to look into our company’s existing research and conduct some of my own. I discovered BlueJay was still looking for their target audience, while talking with the business. We set up a meeting and discussed their business needs, their target audiences, as well as an overview of the United States’ healthcare system’s situation regarding both parties:

After discussing these topics, we narrowed down our target audience: Private outpatient physical therapist clinics and practitioners were their primary target audience.

Research

I immediately scheduled interviews with several of our beta testers and with several private physical therapy clinics who expressed interest in a mobile physical therapy tool. From these, I interviewed 10 physical therapists and clinic owners.

Ideate & Learn

We broke into teams of two to quickly produce 2 concepts:

  • One concept focused on gamifying the experience, which encouraged users to return more often to the website
  • The other developed a more social feature including in-person and virtual meetups and the ability to communicate with the startups.

My group worked on the gamification concept wireframes. I worked on wireframing & prototyping the gamification version with my partner Joel. We wanted both sides to be engaged, so we allowed investors to be awarded badges for certain actions and visually see how close their invested startups were to their funding goal with mountains.

Refining & Results

In the end, after evaluating all of the research including a satisfaction survey, results showed that users liked both ideas but from the interviews, but seemed more excited about the gamification option. We decided to include the parts of the virtual meet up app into the gamification version to try and appeal even more to our target demographic.

Insights

Further Considerations:
  • Work on the entrepreneur portion

    Given the time, we couldn’t pull enough resources to create a quality product for both the entrepreneur and investor portions of the website. If I had more time, I would do more research, wireframe, and test the entrepreneur portion.

What I Learned
  • Have a plan but be prepared for anything

    I learned that sometimes it’s impossible to complete the ideal amount and quality of work in your vision. Both me and Megan, having a project management background, set on having our team finish our set deliverables every morning. However, we quickly learned that it was impossible to use all of the UX tools midway through the project without working 80+ hour weeks. Our team were tired and stressed as a result; so our team then decided to focus only on the essential ux tools in our arsenal.

  • Prioritize Simplicity

    Sometimes you had to kill your babies. There were times we had to discard several ideas, not because they weren’t good, but because we needed to get things done. We realized we would create a quality product if we focused on a few things. And as a result, we learned to focus on only the key components of either the design process or our site’s features.
