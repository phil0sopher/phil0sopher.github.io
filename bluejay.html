Background

During my time at BlueJay Mobile Health, I was tasked with solving the digital health business’ and their users’ needs. During this time:

  1. I conducted and synthesized both user and marketing research
  2. I designed the payment flow of the product and marketing material
  3. I designed and developed their company’s website framework

Getting Started

Since I was new to the company and our audience, I decided to look into our company’s existing research and conduct some of my own. I discovered BlueJay was still looking for their target audience, while talking with the business. We set up a meeting and discussed their business needs, their target audiences, as well as an overview of the United States’ healthcare system’s situation regarding both parties:

After discussing these topics, we narrowed down our target audience: Private outpatient physical therapist clinics and practitioners were their primary target audience.

Research

I immediately scheduled interviews with several of our beta testers and with several private physical therapy clinics who expressed interest in a mobile physical therapy tool. From these, I interviewed 10 physical therapists and clinic owners.

Define

Using the data I gathered and synthesized from our interviews, I designed our main persona and several proto personas:

During this stage we decided to focus solely on the physical therapist's side because physical therapists needed to be on the platform first so they can schedule in their patients, as well as vice-versa.

Ideate & Learn

I discussed with my team and decided that my priorities were to redesign and to create new pages for the existing website, conduct usability studies on the existing app, and design screens for the payment flow of the app.

Payment Flow

Usability Results

We tested primary user's most vital flows: adding a patient list, prescribing treatment plans to patients, and conducting virtual visit.

Key Metrics

We tested the vital flows on several potential users and found that our metrics showed: 4 errors made during task completion as well as a long average of 25 minutestime to create and submit a treatment plan

Satisfaction Survey

We found 40% would not recommend the existing app to a friend

Key Feedback
  •  “I like how easy it is to add a patient to my dashboard.”

  •  “'adding an exercise' is confusing for me, I've been trying to find this exercise for the longest time”

  •  “I didn't want to end the video visit because I didn’t want to lose my notes”

Temporary Pages(Coded)

For these pages, I added the HIPPA Compliance page, updated every single page styles, cleaned up the code to fit proper semantics, and made the pages responsive so that potential users would be able to acccess the website on their phone after learning about the product at a conference.

Refining & Results

In the end, after evaluating all of the research including a satisfaction survey, results showed that users liked the idea but wanted a more streamlined approach. So the company set to redesigning the product.

We also made redesigning the website a priority, since the Combined Sections Meeting by the American Physical Therapy Association was coming up in the next month, by taking my existing designs and redesigning the website using Webflow.

Redesigned Website

Before my contract ended, I created a basic framework on Webflow; this lead to the current website's design.

Insights

Further Considerations:
  • Completely redesign the existing flow

    Given the time and resources, we couldn’t pull enough resources to revamp the existing flow. Instead we pinpointed the existing issues and tried to solve them, while keeping in mind we would need to do an overhaul of the existing app in the future.

What I Learned
  • Teamwork is key

    I learned that working with the engineers and keeping them up to date helps everyone. They would give me great feedback which could change the designs based on viability and easy of implementation.

  • Finding balance

    There were times I needed to take a step back in my work-life balance. I'm glad that I noticed whenever I would become too tunnel-visioned in a design and needed to take a step back because those were the moments which led to the most crucial decisions.
